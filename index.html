This website shows the sun and moon positions in the sky at Halifax, Nova Scotia, for the present day. The outer circle represents the horizon, and letters indicate (true, not magnetic) directions North, East, etc. The vertical and horizontal lines intersect at the zenith, i.e. the point directly overhead. The sun trajectory is drawn in red, while blue is used for the moon. The numbers indicate the hours of the day, in a 24-hour clock set to local standard time (that is, not taking any account of daylight-savings time shifts).

R code to create this graph and others like it is provided in the sunmoon4Halifax.R script, which is available at GitHub. If you would like to explore the variation at other times of the year, or at different locations, type 

source("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/dankelley/sunmoon/master/sun_moon_app.R")
shinyApp(ui, server)
in an R or RStudio window, and then use the slider and pulldown menus. (You must first install the R packages shiny and oce, for this to work.) If you would like to have another location added to the pull-down menu, please feel free to email me at dan.kelley@dal.ca and I will add your suggestion.
